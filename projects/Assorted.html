Assorted Projects

DFM covered an immense amount of material - manufacturing proccesses strengths & limitations/design strategies/relative costs, engineering material selection, and how to navigate all these options to optimize a part/assembly of parts' functionality and price point. We covered GD&T and how to create fully-dimensioned, readable drawings. It was hands down one of the most information-dense courses I've ever taken.

The course deliverables consisted of two immense project design reports. For the first, each group had to selected an existing product with 15+ parts and reverse-engineer it completely: model all components, create fully dimensioned drawings, evaluate each one's manufacturing method/material choice with included cost estimations, and finally provide 3 DFMA improvements to reduce the product's cost without sacrificing functionality. The second report had similar requirements to the reverse engineering report, except each group had to develop a novel product in this case (total part count was flexible, and there were no DFMA improvements to make).
