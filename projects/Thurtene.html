Thurtene

The ThurtenE Carnival is a strange but enduring philanthropic tradition at WashU. Students form into 6 massive teams and compete to build the best themed carnival facade/theater. The carnival is a popular weekend for the community and alumni. WashU grads and St. Louisans alike flock to the event to watch the performances and donate to their favorite facade. Proceeds go to each group's partner charity of choice.

While my involvement with ThurtenE spanned my entire college career, I served as our group's project director during my last year. The job was equal parts project management, architectural design, and construction workhorse. I managed 200 volunteers in all design and construction tasks for the 1000+ man-hour project over the course of 6 months.

Our theme was 'City Block'. We built the outside to resemble urban store fronts while the inside imitated a subway station. The facade was a hit and we were awarded first place overall!

Related Skills: Project Management, CAD, Woodshop, Laser Cutting, Graphic Design, Design Sketching
