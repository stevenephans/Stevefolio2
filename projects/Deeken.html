Wash U Medical School

MATLAB + Rapid Prototyping + Data Analytics + Machine Shop + Wood Shop

During the Summer of 2014, I worked on a interdisciplinary team of engineers, surgeons, and computer scientists to develop an orignal mesh material for hernia repair surgeries which more naturally replicated abdominal tissue material properties. I was primarily responsible for designing and building experimental setups critical to research data acquisition. Using fluoroscopic imaging, we tracked in-vivo movement of metallic tissue markers. Then using Direct Linear Transformation algorithms, we calculated distance and backed out material property estimates. The equipment I built served as a 3D cartesian grid which we used as a fixed frame of reference. We were a small team, and besides experimental setup design, I supported the work however I could by helping with data analysis, MATLAB scripting, and more.
