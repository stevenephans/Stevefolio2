Design For Manufacturing

CAD + DFMA + GD&T + Technical Writing + Product Design

DFM taught me an immense amount of material - manufacturing proccesses strengths & limitations/design strategies/relative costs, engineering material selection, and how to navigate all these options to optimize a part/assembly of parts' functionality and price point. We also covered GD&T and how to create fully-dimensioned, readable drawings. It was hands down one of the most information-dense courses I've ever taken.

The course deliverables were two comprehensive engineering design reports. For the first, each group selected an existing product with 15+ parts and reverse-engineered it completely: model all components, draft manufacturing drawings, evaluate the production process (material choices, manufacturing methods, assembly procedures), and advise 3 DFMA improvements to reduce cost without sacrificing functionality. The second report required similar analysis, but for a novel product design developed by the group.
