Design For Manufacturing

CAD, DFMA, GD&T, Product Design, Project Management

DFM covered an immense amount of material - manufacturing proccesses strengths & limitations/design strategies/relative costs, engineering material selection, and how to navigate all these options to optimize a part/assembly of parts' functionality and price point. We covered GD&T and how to create fully-dimensioned, readable drawings. It was hands down one of the most information-dense courses I've ever taken.

The course deliverables were two comprehensive engineering design reports. For the first, each group selected an existing product with 15+ parts and reverse-engineered it completely: model all components, draft manufacturing drawings, fully evaluate the production process (manufacturing methods, material choices, product assembly), and finally provide 3 DFMA improvements to reduce cost without sacrificing product functionality. The second report required similar analysis, but concerned a novel product design developed by the group.

Reverse Engineering Project

Design Thinking is a human-centered approach to product development. Before any ideation took place, we needed to understand our user's point-of-view. Why don't they always pick up after their pets? What's the worst bit about the clean-up process? We interviewed numerous dog owners in the Boulder area, listening to their views on the issue and looking for hidden insights and common themes.
