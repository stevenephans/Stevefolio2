#HashtagRace

HTML + CSS + GRAPHIC DESIGN

In February of 2020, some friends and I participated in CU Boulder's bi-annual hackathon put on by Major League Hacking. Hack CU was my first ever hackathon, and seeing as I had never programmed a line of code in my life as of August 2019 I was both nervous and excited to see how I could help my team (who are all Computer Science Master's students). The contest was completely open ended, but each event sponsor had a challenge for teams to pursue. We opted to compete in Twitter's (best use of the Twitter API) and Adobe's (best use of Adobe XD). After an hour or two of brainstorming, we decided to build a webgame inspired by Wikirace, except in ours you would have to navigate from a start to an end hashtag solely using other hashtags in the available tweets. We dubbed it '#HASHTAGRACE'.

Besides helping develop the idea, I designed the UX with Adobe Xd and coded the entire front-end. As the team finished pieces of the back-end, I supported its integration into my code. 23.5 hours after starting we had a working prototype with two modes: you could try and link the chosen hashtags manually, or you could let 'Bob the Bot' try and find a connection. He often did, it was often hilarious, and it was often not PG. Twitter can be a dark place...

While Twitter's judges didn't love Bob's creative connections, the Adobe Team was thrilled with our use of XD. We were the only group to build our UX design into a working website. They awarded us a free year-long subscription to Creative Cloud for the project. Our group's entry also won 'Most Random' in the overall competition.

Unfortunately, Twitter has since implemented some serious adjustments to their API which broke the #HashtagRace's back-end. At some point we hope to sit down as a team and re-work the code to get it up and running again.

Our user-side Adobe Xd mock-up. As you make choices, the most common neighbor hashtags auto-populate the screen. Click count is tracked in the bottom left so you can compare your performance against Bob the Bot. If and when you successfully link from start to end, the UI pulls and displays the Tweets used. All hashtags used in the UI mock-up are completely made-up visual placeholders...in case that was not obvious.

Winner winner chicken dinner.
