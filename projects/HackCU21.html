Launch Party

HTML + CSS + JAVASCRIPT + GRAPHIC DESIGN

When my friends asked me to once again join their Hack CU team I readily accepted. With fully remote operation, the 2021 hackathon didn't attract as many participants or sponsors, but there were still some exciting challenges and prizes on the table. We opted to compete for 'Best UI/UX' with our project - LaunchParty.

Launch Party is a dedicated platform for music fans to digitally commune and listen to new album drops for the first time together. The application's lets fans react positively/negatively to songs in real time as they are listening and rate each song as well as the album as a whole based-off their first impressions. After completing the first listen, they then can access a dedicated page to discuss the album in general, individual songs, and view aggregate stats with everyone else who has listened.

Similar to the first hackathon, my teammates handled all back-end programming and I handled the front-end code, UI, and UX. It was an exhausting 24 hours, but we delivered an impressively complete MVP and won the 'Best UI/UX' contest!

MVP With Working Spotify Account Link

Here is our final deliverable after 24 long hours of work. The MVP pulls all song information from the linked account in real time using the Spotify API. The waveform visualization color reflects the user's reaction clicks - minty green for positive feedback and grey for negative. On the back end, all of the reaction information is time-stamped and stored in a database to eventually be translated into song specific infographics. Finally, when the song approaches completion, the MVP prompts you to rate the song.

Adobe Xd UI Mock-Up

Vanilla JS Prototype

We built the MVP's front-end using the two interface protoypes above as reference. The Xd mock-up on the left provides a more complete view of the UI/UX flow, including a bare bones example of what the post-listen album stats page might look like. While the MVP is more polished than the JavaScript prototype on the right in almost every way, the prototype shows the reaction depletion feature in action. We wanted users to react thoughtfully rather than just spam the buttons out of control (which inevitably some people would do), so we opted for a finite amount per song. Each button's background fill displays the amount remaining, and the button is disabled once fully depleted.
