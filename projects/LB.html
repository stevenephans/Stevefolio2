Longboard Building

Product Design + Wood Shop + Graphic Design + AutoCAD

I started longboarding in the 6th grade - around the same time my dad granted me supervision-free access to his rudimentary garage wood shop. Skating quickly became my go-to after school activity, and it wasn't long before I stared building boards myself.

Longboard building was my unofficial introduction to engineering and product development. Over the course of 5 or so years, my process evolved from basic plywood cutouts, to laminated composite decks pressed into curved profiles. I started ordering single-ply hardwood sheets in bulk from Canadian sawmills so I could customize layer-to-layer grain alignment, and rolls of tri-axial fiberglass cloth to reduce deck thickness while improving strength and flexibility. At its peak, pretty much every penny I earned working at the neighborhood pizza parlor went to board building R&D. I never actually bought a board after my very first one...so I guess I was saving some money in that respect?
